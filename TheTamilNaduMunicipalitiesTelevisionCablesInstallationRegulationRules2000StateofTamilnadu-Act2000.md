The Tamil Nadu Municipalities Television Cables Installation Regulation Rules,
2000Published vide Notification G.O. Ms. No. 58, Municipal Administration and
Water Supply Department, dated 4th May 2000, Published in Part 3-Section 1 (a)
of the Tamil Nadu Government Gazette, Extraordinary dated the 5th May 2000.
Issue No.295 (pages 1-5)S.R.O. No. A-40 (a)/2000. - In exercise of the powers
conferred by section 347 of the Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919
(Tamil Nadu Act IV of 1919), section 303 of the Tamil Nadu District
Municipalities Act, 1920 (Tamil Nadu Act V of 1920), section 431 of the
Madurai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 15 of 1971),
section 430 of the Coimbatore City Municipal Corporation Act, 1981 (Tamil Nadu
Act 25 of 1981), read with section 8 of the Tiruchirapalli City Municipal
Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 27 of 1994), the Tirunelveli City
Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 28 of 1994) and the Salem City
Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 29 of 1994), the Governor of
Tamil Nadu hereby makes the following rules, namely:-

### 1. Short title and commencement.

(1)These, rules maybe called the Tamil Nadu Municipalities Television Cables
Installation Regulation Rules, 2000.(2)These rules shall apply to all town
Panchayats, municipalities and municipal corporations in the State.(3)It shall
come into force on the 5th May 2000.

### 2. Definitions.

\- In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires,-(a)"Act" means the
Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919 (Tamil Nadu Act IV of 1919), the
Tamil Nadu District Municipalities Act, 1920 (Tamil Nadu Act V of 1920), the
Madurai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1971 (Tamil Nadu Act 15 of 1971), the
Coimbatore City Municipal Corporation Act, 1981 (Tamil Nadu Act 25 of 1981),
the Tiruchirapalli City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 27 of
1994), the Tirunelveli City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 28
of 1994), the Salem City Municipal Corporation Act, 1994 (Tamil Nadu Act 29 of
1994), as the case may be;(b)"council" means the council of town Panchayats,
municipalities and municipal corporations, as the case may
be;(c)"Commissioner" means the Commissioners of municipalities and municipal
corporations and the Executive Officers of the town Panchayat;(d)"Form" means
the forms appended to these rules;(e)"Government" means the State
Government;(f)"municipality" with its grammatical variation means the
municipal corporations, municipalities and town Panchayats constituted or
deemed to have been constituted under the appropriate Act;(g)"television
cables" means the cables used by any private individual or firm or company to
give satellite television connection in the televisions used by the public in
a particular area;(h)words and expressions used but not defined in these rules
shall have the meanings, respectively, assigned to them in the Act. In the
absence of such meaning, the Tamil Nadu General Clauses Act, 1891 (Tamil Nadu
Act I of 1891) shall apply for the interpretation of these rules.

### 3. Issue of provisional permission for installation of television cables
over public roads and streets in a municipality.

(1)Any person intending to commence operation of giving cable television
connections in any area within the municipal limits, shall apply to the
Commissioner in Part I of Form I for provisional permission to install cables
on street light poles over public roads and streets or to erect separate poles
in any road or streets within the municipal limit for installing such
television cables.(2)Every application under sub-rule (1) for a provisional
permission shall be accompanied by-(a)a sketch indicating the roads and
streets over which the cables are to be installed;(b)the number of street
light poles which are to be used for installing the cables; and(c)the numbers
of additional poles required to be erected by the applicant on the roads and
streets for installing such cables.(3)The Commissioner shall, on receipt of
application, scrutinise it and if satisfied about the information furnished by
the applicant, grant a provisional permission in Part II of Form I, after
receiving the annual rent for such installation of television cables in
accordance with rule 4, and subject to the following conditions,
namely:-(a)The television cables shall be installed over the public roads and
streets at a height of 8 metres and above across the roads, or streets and at
a height of 5.5 metres and above along the roads or streets. The cable shall
be at least 1 metre away from the light fixture;(b)Not more than five lines
shall be taken on each of the street light poles;(c)As far as practicable and
possible, the street light poles of the urban local bodies may be used;(d)If
additional poles are required to be erected on any public road or street or
land, the prior concurrence of the Commissioner shall be obtained.(4)Within
sixty days from the date of receipt of such provisional permission, the
applicant shall install the cables in accordance with the conditions
stipulated in the provisional permission and shall apply in Part 1 of Form II
for issue of a No Objection Certificate by the Commissioner of the
municipality.(5)On satisfying himself that the cables have been installed in
accordance with the conditions mentioned in the provisional permission under
sub-rule (1), and subject to the provisions contained in the Act, the
Commissioner may issue a No Objection Certificate in Part II of Form II.

### 4. Rent to be collected for installation of television cable.

(1)The annual rent to be collected by the municipality for using the street
light poles and for erecting additional poles by the applicants shall be-

(a) Chennai City Municipal Corporation. |  Rs. 9400 (Rupees nine thousand and fourhundred) per annum per kilometer.  
---|---  
(b) All City Municipal Corporations other thanChennai City Municipal Corporation. |  Rs. 6300 (Rupees six thousand and three hundred)per annum per kilometre.  
(c) Municipalities and Town Panchayats. Rs. 5500 |  (Rupees five thousand and five hundred) perannum per kilometre.  
  
(2)For the purpose of collecting annual rent to a period relating to less than
a year, the annual rent fixed under sub-rule (1) shall be collected for a
period of six months.

### 5. Removal of television cables from the street light poles for violation
of conditions imposed.

(1)Where the Commissioner either suo motu or on written representation has a
reason to believe that the person to whom a No Objection Certificate issued,
has violated or failed to comply with the conditions imposed while issuing the
No Objection Certificate under these rules, he shall call upon the person in
writing stating the grounds to show cause within such period not less than
fifteen days from the date of receipt of such notice as to why the No
Objection Certificate issued by the Commissioner should not be cancelled and
the television cables should not be removed from the street light poles and
the additional poles, if any, erected and also shall give the person an
opportunity of being heard.(2)On examining the reply received from the person
concerned within the time limit specified in sub-rule (1), the Commissioner
may order cancellation of No Objection Certificate and for removal of the
television cables installed by the person concerned within the time limit
fixed anti failing which the Commissioner shall remove them by utilising the
services of the employees of the municipality.

### 6. Mode of payment of rent.

\- The annual rent for installation of television cables shall be paid in the
form of bank draft payable to the commissioner or shall be paid in cash in the
municipal treasury.

### 7. Period of payment of annual rent and levy of fine for belated payment.

(1)The annual rent for using the street light poles and for erecting
additional poles for installation of television cables shall be paid by the
persons concerned within the period from the 1st April to the 30th April of
every year failing which a sum of rupee one per month for the annual rent of
every one hundred rupees shall be collected:Provided that for determining the
fine, fraction of more than fifty rupees shall be rounded off to the next
hundred rupees and any fraction less than fifty' rupees shall be
ignored.(2)The payment of annual rent from the 1st day of calendar month to
the end of that calendar month after expiry of the due dates fixed under sub-
rule (1) shall be taken as one month for levy of fine.

### 8. Installation of brackets in the street light poles by the municipality.

(1)The Commissioner shall make arrangements to install brackets to have the
television cables in the street light poles in such a way that the brackets
can hold five television cable lines.(2)In respect of the existing street
light poles, the Commissioner shall provide such brackets on the street light
pole within a period of one year from the date of coming into force of these
rules.(3)While providing brackets in the street light poles under sub-rule
(2), priority shall be given to the street light poles in roads and streets
where there are already cables in existence.(4)The commissioner may provide
such brackets in the new street light poles to be installed in future.

### 9. Issue of No Objection Certificate for the existing cable television
operators.

\- The cable television operators who are operating the cable television on
the date of coming into force of these rules shall apply in Part I of Form II
fulfilling the conditions given in sub-rule (3) of rule 3 and on payment of
rent as prescribed in rule 4 and shall obtain a No Objection Certificate from
the Commissioner within a period of six months from the date of coming into
force of these rules.

### 10. Interpretation of these rules by the Government.

(1)If any question arises as to interpretation of these rules, the question
shall be referred to the Government and whose decision shall be final.(2)If
any difficulty arises as to giving effect to the provision of these rules, the
Government may, by order, do any thing which appears to be necessary, for the
purpose of removing the difficulty.Form I
