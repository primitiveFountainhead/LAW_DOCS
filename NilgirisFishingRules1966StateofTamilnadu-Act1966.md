exercise of the powers conferred by sections 4 and 5 of the Nilgiris Game and
Fish Preservation Act, 1875 (Tamil Nadu Act II of 1879); sections 26,33 and 63
of the Tamil Nadu Forest Act, 1882 (Tamil Nadu Act V of 1882), and section 6
of the Indian Fisheries Act, 1897 (Central Act IV of 1897) and in supersession
of the Nilgiris Fishing Rules published with the late Development Department
Notification No. Nil, dated 23rd October 1953, published at pages 378-381 of
Rules Supplement to Part I of the Fort St. George Gazette, dated 18th
November, 1953, as subsequently amended, the Governor of Tamil Nadu hereby
makes the following rules the same having been previously published for
general information:-

### 1. Short title, application and direction.

(a)These rules may be called the Nilgiris Fishing Rules, 1966.(b)They shall
apply to the waters within the area specified in Schedule I to these
rules;(c)These rules shall remain in force for a period of fifteen to twenty
seven years from the date of the publication in the Official Gazette.

### 2. Definitions.

\- In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires,-(a)"Assistant
Director" means the Assistant Director of Fisheries having jurisdiction over
the Nilgiris District;(b)"Director" means the Director of Fisheries, [Chennai]
[Substituted for the word 'Madras' by the City of Madras (Alteration of Name)
Act, 1996 (Tamil Nadu Act 28 of 1996).];(c)"Fishing" means and includes
capture of, or attempt to capture, fish by any means including hooks and
lines, fixed engines, curves, weirs or nets or by damming or baling of any
water;(d)"Government" means the Government of Tamil Nadu;(e)"Inspector" means
the Inspector of Fisheries.

### 3. Waters in which fishing is controlled by the Department of Fisheries.

\- The Fisheries Department shall control fishing in all the waters specified
in Schedules II to IV to these rules.

### 4. Prohibition of erection or use of fixed engines, etc., and fishing.

\- No person shall-(a)erect or use any fixed engine or net for capturing
fish;(b)use any poison or throw any toxic matter likely to result in fish
mortality;(c)use dynamite or other explosive substance for fishing;(d)fish in
any other method in the waters within the area specified in Schedule I to
these rules except under, and in accordance with, the terms and conditions of
a licence issued in the Form set out in Schedule V to these rules.

### 5. Authority to issue licences and the period.

(1)Fishing licences shall be issued by any officer of the Fisheries Department
not below the rank of an Assistant Inspector of Fisheries or any other person,
or association authorised in this behalf by the Director.(2)The period covered
by a licence shall be clearly endorsed on the licence.

### 7. Cancellation or refusal.

\- The Officer issuing the licence may cancel the licence or refuse to issue a
further licence to any person who fails to comply with the provisions of these
rules.

### 8. Fees not to be refunded.

\- No refund of the licence fees paid shall be made on any category of
licence, (whether used or unused or cancelled), unless the licence is
surrendered at least two clear days in advance of the date of commencement of
the licence.

### 9. Furnishing of information by persons holding monthly and annual
licences.

\- Every holder of a monthly or annual licence shall send a report in the Form
specified in Schedule VI to these rules within seven days of the close of each
calendar month during the period of validity of the licence.

### 10. Time for fishing.

\- No licensee shall fish before 6 a.m. and after 7 p.m.

### 11. Rules for trout fishing.

(a)Trout fishing shall be allowed in all the waters specified in Schedule II
to these rules.(b)Fishing in the waters specially reserved for trout fishing,
and in the tributaries of such waters shall be, subject to the following
provisions, namely:-(i)The holder of a trout fishing licence may also take any
other fish he may catch in the said waters. He may also fish in the waters
mentioned in Schedule 111 to these rules, subject to the rules regulating
fishing in these waters, but without alterations of the maximum catch of trout
allowed on his trout licence, the holder of an annual licence for mixed
fishing may, on payment of an additional sum of Rs. 50 (Rupees fifty only) per
year or such other sum as may be fixed, from time to time, by the Director,
obtain a yearly licence for trout fishing.(ii)Fishing shall be closed in these
waters including tributaries of such waters during such periods-as may, from
time to time, be notified by the Director in the Nilgiris and Coimbatore
District Gazette.(iii)Fishing in the waters specially reserved for trout
fishing or in the tributaries of such waters shall be with artificial fly or
flies on a single hook only. No weight in any form shall be attached to the
cast, fly or line. Spinning may be permitted in such water or waters by the
Director in writing.(iv)For the purpose of this rules, "spinning" means the
use of artificial minnows, lure or spoon and dead bait (which means dead fish
only) used with spinner or by attaching to the hook or hooks a dead fish so
placed (i.e. by arranging the tail) as to spin in the water. The use of live
bait is strictly prohibited. On no account shall unrequired bail dead or
alive, be thrown into the lakes.(v)Trolling in the Kukurti, Emerald, Avalanche
and Upper Bhavani Reservoirs, within the limits specified on the notice boards
in the office of the Assistant Director or Inspector, is permitted during such
period as spinning is permitted.(vi)All fishes of a length less than that
fixed, from time to time, by the Director should be returned alive to the
waters where they have been captured.(vii)Every licence holder should carry a
disgorger.(viii)All trout bearing marks by the Fisheries Department of the
Government for experimental purposes should, after capture, be surrendered
intact to the nearest Fisheries Officer with information regarding the place,
date and time of capture.

### 13. Issue of more than one licence.

(1)No person shall take more than three annual licences in a year for fishing
in any of the waters.(2)The Director may vary the maximum number and size of
trout which can be captured with a trout licence of any description in such
manner and for such period as he thinks fit.

### 14. Rules for mixed fishing (trout and carps).

(1)Fishing any species of fish with one rod and a single or triple hook bait
or lure shall be permitted in the waters mentioned in Schedule III to these
rules which are not reserved for trout fishing, but in which trout may also be
available.(2)The fees for such licences and the number of trouts which a

### 15. Rules for carp fishing.

(1)Carp fishing shall be allowed in the waters mentioned in Schedule IV to
these rules.(2)The following fees shall be charged for licences for fishing in
such waters with one rod and any bait lure or hook (except in the case of
masheer for which triple hook is permitted).(i)Daily-Rs. 3 (Rupees Three
only).(ii)Monthly-(30 days from the date of issue) - Rs. 30 (Rupees Thirty
only).(iii)Annual (12 months from the date of issue)-Rs. 75 (Rupees Seventy-
five only).

### 16. General conditions of licence for trout, mixed and carp fishing.

(1)Licences issued are not transferable and shall be shown on demand to any
Fisheries, Forest, Revenue or Police Officer or any person authorised by the
Director.(2)Licence holders shall, on demand, show their catches to any or all
of the above officers or persons.

### 17. Cancellation or refusal of licence for conviction or other reasons.

\- The officer issuing licences may refuse to grant a licence under these
rules to any applicant who has been convicted of an offence against the
provision of these rules or for any other reason to be stated in writing and
may, on, similar grounds, cancel any licence already granted. When a licence
is so cancelled, the fees paid for the licence shall not be refunded.

### 18. No refund of fee if declared to be closed for fishing.

\- If during the currency of any category of licence, any water or waters or
portions of waters thereof either specially reserved for trout fishing or
otherwise are declared as closed for fishing for any reason or purpose
whatsoever, the holder of a licence for fishing in such waters shall have no
claim of refund of the licence fee or any portion thereof in respect of the
licence held by him.

### 19. Prohibition of the use of more than one rod.

\- The use of more than one rod at a time under one licence is prohibited. For
the purposes of this rule, a rod which is not in the licence holder's hand,
but which is left with its line in the water shall be considered to be a rod
in use.

### 20. Powers of the Director.

(1)The Director may, by notification in the Nilgiris and Coimbatore District
Gazette,-(i)declare, from time to time, that all or any of the waters whether
reserved for trout fishing or not are closed or open to fishing;
or(ii)prohibit the fishing of any particular species of fish in all or any or
portions of any or all waters for any specified period.(2)Notwithstanding
anything contained in these rules, the Director may, by notification in the
Gazettes of Nilgiris and Coimbatore districts,-(i)reduce or increase the rate
of fee for a trout or mixed or carp fishing licence;(ii)decide the method of
catching trout or other species of fishes;(iii)permit spinning or other
methods of fishing in any of the waters reserved for trout fishing;(iv)declare
any trout water as mixed fishing water and any mixed fishing waters as trout
fishing water;(v)permit or disallow the use of power boats or other kind of
boats in any of the waters, subject to general or special concurrence of the
Tamil Nadu State Electricity Board or any officer authorised by the Board for
giving such concurrence;(vi)permit any method considered necessary to
eradicate undesirable fish in any of the waters;(vii)permit the use of fixed
engines in any or all waters for the purpose of fishing;(viii)fix hours for
fishing;(ix)approve the forms of fishing licences;(xi)authorise any person or
body or group of persons other than the Fisheries Officers to issue any or all
licences for fishing and prescribe the procedure and conditions for issue of
licences by such persons or organisation.

### 21. Penalties.

(1)Whoever commits a breach of the rules hereinbefore contained shall be
punishable with fine which may extend to one hundred rupees and when the
breach is a continuing breach with a further fine, which may extend to ten
rupees for every day after the date of the first conviction during which the
breach is proved to have been persisted in.(2)A Magistrate may, on the
conviction of any person for the breach of these rules, order the seizure,
forfeiture and removal of fixed engines erected or used or nets used in
contravention of these rules and also order the forfeiture of any fish taken
by means of any such fixed engine or net.(3)Whoever commits breach of rules,
4,11,12,14,15 and 16 shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which
may extend to one month or with fine which may extend to two hundred rupees or
with both.

### 22. Persons appointed to enforce these rules to be deemed to be Forest
Officers.

\- Every person appointed or authorised by, or acting under the orders of the
Director for the purpose of enforcing these rules shall be deemed to be a
Forest Officer under the Tamil Nadu Forest Act, 1882 (Tamil Nadu Act V of
1882).

### 23. Savings.

\- Nothing contained in these rules shall apply to fishing conducted by, or
under the orders of, the Director or any other Fisheries Officer empowered by
the Director in this regard.
